Fun Facts!

Development & Casting

  • Joey and Monica: Joey and Monica were originally intended to be the central "will-they-won't-they" couple of the show. Monica and Chandler's relationship was originally planned as a one-time hookup in London, but audience reaction was so strong that creators made them a permanent couple.
  • Names before Friends:Before landing on Friends, the show went through several working titles, including Insomnia Café, Friends Like Us, Six of One, and Across the Hall.
  • Different Castings: Courteney Cox was originally offered the role of Rachel but requested to play Monica because she liked the "stronger" character. David Schwimmer was the only actor who didn't have to audition, as the role of Ross was written specifically for him.
  • Famous Rejections: Ellen DeGeneres turned down the role of Phoebe, while Kathy Griffin and Jane Lynch both auditioned for the part.

Production Secrets

  • Stuck in One Location: The famous episode "The One Where No One's Ready" takes place entirely in Monica's apartment because the production ran out of money and couldn't afford additional sets or guest stars.
  • Courteney Cox's Marriage:In the Season 6 premiere, "The One After Vegas," the entire cast had "Arquette" added to their names in the opening credits to celebrate Courteney Cox’s marriage to David Arquette.
  • Unseen Actor: Despite being a recurring joke for years, the identity of the actor who played "Ugly Naked Guy" (Jon Haugen) was not officially revealed until 2016.

Cast Solidarity

  • Equal Pay: The Friends cast was the first in TV history to negotiate their salaries as a group. They insisted on equal pay for all six members, eventually reaching $1 million per episode each for the final season.
  • Group Rituals: Before every single taping, the cast would hold a private huddle backstage to get into character and wish each other a good show.

Potential Endings

  • Joey and Phoebe: Matt LeBlanc and Lisa Kudrow pitched an ending where it's revealed that the two had been together secretly for all ten seasons, but the writers felt it was "too messy" for the finale's tone.
  • Phoebe's Husband: The writers were genuinely split 50/50 on who Phoebe should marry. They filmed the finale without a firm plan for her husband until Paul Rudd's massive popularity tipped the scales over Hank Azaria.
  • Alternate Paris Ending: One draft had Ross moving to France to be a stay-at-home dad while Rachel pursued her career, rather than Rachel "getting off the plane" and giving up her dream job in Paris.
  • Rachel and Ross Undefined: The creators briefly toyed with a "realistic" ending where Ross and Rachel merely hinted at getting back together in the future, but they ultimately decided the fans would "revolt" without a definitive romantic payoff.
